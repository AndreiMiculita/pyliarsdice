The game consists of multiple rounds. In every round, all players shake their cups and place them down, such that the dice are only visible to themselves.

Source: gamesforyoungminds.com

Then, one of the players (in particular, the player who has lost the previous round) starts with a bid. In a clock-wise fashion, we go to the next player's turn. Except for the first turn in the round, every player has a choice between two actions:
  • Overbid the previous player - overbidding means that either the number of dice is higher than the bid of the previous player, or the value of the die is higher than that of the previous player (with the same number of dice).
    Examples:
    • Correct: 3 ×
      → 4 ×
      (Higher number of dice)
    • Correct: 3 ×
      → 3 ×
      (Higher value with the same number of dice)
    • Incorrect: 3 ×
      → 2 ×
      (Lower number of dice)
    • Incorrect: 3 ×
      → 3 ×
      (Lower value with the same number of dice)
  • Overbidding with Joker dice - a joker die (equivalent to the "1" face) counts as double the number of dice when it's used as a bid. This is because every other value has a double chance of appearing on the table (since a joker also counts towards their number), while the joker itself is only based on the probability of appearing itself.

A bid can be anything, independently of the roll of dice under the cup. This means bluffing is a substantial part of this game and can be strategically used to cause other players to lie.

Source: emojipedia

  • Calling the previous player a liar - whenever you believe that the bid of the previous player is a lie (i.e. the number of dice of the bid is not on the table), you can call that player a liar.

This is what the button for calling a player's bluff looks like. You can't press it here.

When someone has called the previous player a liar, everyone else has to decide, in a clock-wise fashion, who to side with: the accuser or the accused. Then, everyone opens their cup and the dice are counted and compared to the bid.

Players opening their cups. Source: thegadgetflow.com

If the number of the bid is equal to or less than the number of dice on the table, the bid is correct. Keep in mind that Joker dice will always count toward this total. However, if the number of the bid is greater than the number of the dice on the table, the bid is a lie. All the players who are correct lose one die.

Oh no! Someone was caught bluffing. Source: 888poker.com

After this, the round has ended, and a new round starts with the remaining number of dice for each player, restarting the bidding with the player who lost the last round. This will be the person who was either correctly called a liar, or the player who incorrectly called another player a liar. After every round, all the players who were correct lose a die. The goal is to be the first to lose all of the dice from your cup. However, the game will be played until all players except one have no more dice remaining their cups.
